It is interesting to note that way back then, they had the idea to lease an office/laboratory in a large building on the wharf and that this served to create an interest in its field of operations among the Gloucester fishermen. Isnt this what we should do? Set up operations at all the major fishing harbors in the Northeast? JiM suggest finding abandoned/unused vessels and turning them into offices/meeting spaces where we can all collaborate.

George and Huanxin went to the Lowell Instruments workshop on Wednesday to work on unifying our software and hardware platforms. Nick and Joaquim are going to test their software on an eMOLT deckbox and are exploring ways to add satellite communications to the Lowell deck data hubs.

Last Saturday, George went up to Portland, Maine for a meeting hosted by the Maine Lobster Institute. MLI has been funded by the National Science Foundation for a large, multi-pronged effort to study potential impacts of the melting ice sheets in the Arctic on the lobster fishery and fishing communities in eastern Canada and coastal New England. Because the project is so multi-dimensional, scientists from many disciplines were present (geophysicists, oceanographers, biologists, economists, and social scientists) alongside industry partners from the Prince Edward Island Fishermens Association, Fundy North, and the Atlantic Offshore Lobstermens Association. This group is hoping to deploy additional eMOLT systems in the Gulf of Maine and the Gulf of St.Lawrence and assimilate existing eMOLT data to improve their forecasting models. For more information about this project, check out this writeup from the University of Maine.

JiMs idea for floating collaboration spaces could maybe even double as bunks for traveling Downeast and into Atlantic Canada to support this projecthopefully well be able to bring on a few new field technicians through the University of Maine or one of our Canadian academic partners in the near future.

Realtime Summary (Vessels with Rockblock Satellite Transmitters)

This week, we received 21 haul-averaged reports from 5 vessels.

Figure 3 - eMOLT Satellite Transmitter status plot for the previous week. In the figure above, you can see the approximate locations where we received Status Reports (a ping from your system letting us know that its still working) and Data Uploads (actual temperature / depth data collected by your probes). The Status Reports come in every 12 hours or so when a system is powered up. Data Uploads only come in when you haul the probes. If you were out fishing and dont see your approximate locations on here, please reach out because there may be a problem with your system.
